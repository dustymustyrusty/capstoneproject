Air Quality Impact

Rising Dust and Pollution

As the Salton Sea continues to shrink, vast areas of the lakebed are exposed, releasing toxic dust into the air. This dust contains harmful chemicals, pesticides, and heavy metals that contribute to serious health issues for nearby communities.

Health Effects on Residents

Poor air quality in the region has led to a rise in asthma, lung disease, and other respiratory conditions. Children and elderly individuals are particularly vulnerable to these airborne pollutants.

Efforts to Improve Air Quality

Various environmental groups and state agencies are working to mitigate the impact by restoring portions of the lake, planting vegetation to reduce dust, and monitoring air quality levels. However, challenges remain due to funding limitations and climate change.
